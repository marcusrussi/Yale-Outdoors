Yale Outdoors Announcement

week of oct 5, 2014


Good evening, YO panlist!

I'm excited to share with you our biggest announcement of the year thus far. This October break, we'll be sending out five amazing outdoor trips. These trips range from a one day excursion in northwest Connecticut to a full-on five-day trek in the Maine wilderness. There's something for all of you – whether you're looking for a peaceful day in the woods or a rugged, but rewarding challenge in the backcountry.

Demand will be high given the breadth of our offerings, and thus you must apply for a spot on the trip(s) of your interest at the link below to be given consideration. You must apply by this Saturday, 10/11, at 1pm, and you'll hear back two days later on Monday.

Conquering Mount Marcy [wed 10/22 - sat 10/25]

Imagine the rooftop of New York. No, we aren't referencing the Empire State Building-- at 5,343', Mount Marcy of the Adirondack High Peaks is the New York state high point, and this fall break we'll be climbing, scrambling and ice-picking our way to its summit. If you're looking for some real thrills, more intense mountain climbing and breathtaking scenery, this is your trip. While we'll be using crampons, mountaineering boots and ice axes (all rentable from the YO gear room, for no additional fee), prior experience with this gear isn't necessary, but a mountaineering or strong backpacking background is encouraged. Count on leaving early in the morning on Wednesday, 10/22/14, and returning to campus in the evening on Saturday, 10/25/14. Cost will not exceed $135 for YO members, with an additional $10 fee for non-members. Contact leaders alex.roth@yale.edu, emma.spence@yale.edu and david.milewicz@yale.edu with any questions. See you on the mountainside, amongst the clouds.

Grafton Notch Loop, Maine [wed 10/22 - sat 10/25]

This Fall Break, we are headed up north to Maine to hike a portion of the Grafton Notch Loop. We will be backpacking a 21-mile point-to-point route where craggy peaks, mossy forests, and moose abound! Most of the trail was recently constructed and completed in 2007; the remaining portion is one of the hardest sections of the Appalachian Trail. We will be leaving early Wednesday morning (10/22) and returning to campus Saturday evening (10/25). The area is remote and rugged and our hiking days will involve steep ascents and descents. Good physical condition and an enthusiastic attitude towards elevation are a must, although extensive backpacking experience is not required. Cost will be $135 dollars per person, plus personal money for any lobster rolls along the way. Contact Zana Davey at zana.davey@yale.edu or Nate Toppelberg at nathaniel.toppelberg@yale.edu with any questions. Maine, oh Maine are we going to have a great trip!

Our Side of the Catskills [wed 10/22 - sun 10/26]

Come hang out and hike with Maia and Angus in the Catskills of upstate New York! The legendary home of Rip Van Winkle and the setting of My Side of the Mountain, these mountains are the best place to spend Fall Break, whether you’re a History/American Studies/Literature/Humanities/English major or just love to be outside. We’ll leave early Wednesday morning and come back Sunday afternoon for four and half days of colorful trees, sweet views and killer sunsets. Also, waterfalls! Moderate to strenuous hikes paired with great food, friends and views—what more could you ask for? Email angus.mossman@yale.edu and maia.hirschler@yale.edu with questions/comments/concerns.

Backpacking in the Delaware Water Gap [wed 10/22 - sat 10/25]

Join us for beautiful backpacking on the Appalachian Trail in the Delaware Water Gap on the Pennsylvania-New Jersey border! We plan to leave campus early on Wednesday, October 22 and return midday on Saturday October 25. We’ll have four days and three nights of hiking with views and hopefully some beautiful late fall foliage. Meals will be the best of outdoor culinary delights. The 28 mile trip will be strenuous with long days of hiking and some elevation changes. New backpackers with hiking experience are welcome! We are especially looking for people with access to a car. The maximum participation cost is $95 (includes gas, tolls, permits, and food). New YO members will pay an additional $10 for the membership fee. Please email catherine.harmer@yale.edu and jade.harvey@yale.edu with questions.

The Right to Bear Mountain (Day Hike) [wed 10/22]

Looking for something woodsy to do on the first day of Fall Break? Come join Yale Outdoors on a day hike to Bear Mountain, Connecticut’s largest peak at 2,315 feet. Enjoy the fantastic views, Appalachian trail, thick woodlands, and small (but awesome!) streams and waterfalls. We'll be leaving in the morning of October 22, and will be back by the evening. Maximum price $15. Contact patrick.cage@yale.edu with any questions
